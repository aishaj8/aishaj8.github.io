Presented in Session: Physical Processes in Lakes (1). Co-authors: Carlos Alberto Arnillas, Vincent Cheng, and George Arhonditsis (University of Toronto Scarborough). This talk applied continuous wavelet analysis to monthly Great Lakes water level data from 1951 to 2013 to decompose sources of variability in the frequency domain. The analysis identified a dominant and persistent seasonal signal (1-year frequency) alongside a secondary signal near the 8-year frequency that gradually dissipates over the study period. Results suggest that the annual water level maximum is shifting progressively later in the year (~0.09 days/year), with the 8-year oscillation losing amplitude over time. Potential mechanisms driving these changes were discussed in the context of global and regional environmental change.
