Presented in Session 53: Great Lakes Water Level Fluctuations and Water Management. This talk examined the main drivers of water level change in Lake Michigan-Huron using multiple regression models. Evaporation (no lag), inflow (no lag), and outflow (4-month lag) emerged as the dominant predictors across top-ranked models, with the highest-ranked model incorporating all five main predictors: evaporation, inflow, outflow, runoff, and precipitation. In addition to identifying causal drivers, the study addressed the challenge of detecting long-term trends in hydro-meteorological variables against the backdrop of an intensifying hydrological cycle. Non-parametric Mann-Kendall trend analysis revealed significant upward trends in minimum air temperature, increasing evaporation from late spring to early fall, elevated runoff trends in winter, and mostly non-significant trends in over-lake precipitation.
