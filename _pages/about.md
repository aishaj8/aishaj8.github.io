About me 
------

**Feeding the world without costing the planet.**  

Watershed science at the nexus of food, water, and climate.
I am a Post Doctoral Fellow working at the intersection of agricultural sustainability, water quality, and climate science. My research asks a question that sits at the heart of one of this generation's defining challenges: how do we sustain food production for a growing global population without compromising the water and climate systems that make it possible? <br> <br>
My doctoral work at the Univerity of Toronto examined phosphorus and nitrogen export from the Lake Ontario watershed, using the SWAT modelling framework to identify and evaluate catchment-scale mitigation strategies. Alongside this, I explored multi-nutrient dynamics and the integration of farm-scale models to better connect field-level processes with watershed-scale outcomes.<br> <br>
I am currently investigating greenhouse gas emissions — including CO₂, N₂O, and CH₄ — from urban and peri-urban agricultural systems, while maintaining a parallel focus on water quality linkages and nutrient runoff impacts on receiving waterbodies. As cities expand into agricultural land, understanding the full environmental footprint of urban and peri-urban food production is becoming increasingly urgent — for climate policy, water management, and food security alike.<br> <br>
I am always keen to connect with researchers, practitioners, and policymakers working on agricultural sustainability, hydrological modelling, and urban food systems. Feel free to reach out.
