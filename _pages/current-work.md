## What I Am Working On

My postdoctoral research investigates the environmental footprint of urban and peri-urban agriculture — a rapidly growing but poorly understood component of our food system. As cities expand into agricultural land, these farming systems occupy a unique and complex position: they contribute to local food security, but their greenhouse gas emissions and water quality impacts remain largely unquantified.

My work addresses this gap across two interconnected themes:

### Greenhouse Gas Emissions from Urban and Peri-Urban Agriculture

I am quantifying emissions of carbon dioxide (CO₂), nitrous oxide (N₂O), and methane (CH₄) from urban and peri-urban farmland, with the goal of understanding how management practices, land use intensity, and proximity to urban infrastructure shape the climate footprint of these systems. A key question driving this work is whether urban agriculture can be a genuine climate solution — or whether its environmental costs are being underestimated.

### Water Quality Linkages

Alongside the climate focus, I am examining nutrient runoff and its impacts on receiving waterbodies. This thread connects directly to my doctoral work on phosphorus and nitrogen export at the watershed scale, extending those insights into a new and increasingly policy-relevant context. Understanding the water quality implications of urban and peri-urban food production is critical for city planners, water managers, and agricultural policymakers alike.
