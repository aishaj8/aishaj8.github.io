Lake Erie represents a unique case study in which a diverse array of data-driven and process-based models have significantly advanced understanding of major ecosystem processes. This paper presents a technical analysis of recent modelling work conducted to support the adaptive management process in Lake Erie — the most biologically productive system of the Great Lakes. The evaluation examines how well different model types reproduce nutrient loading dynamics, harmful algal bloom formation, and hypoxia patterns, and identifies persistent knowledge gaps and modelling uncertainties that must be addressed to improve management outcomes.
