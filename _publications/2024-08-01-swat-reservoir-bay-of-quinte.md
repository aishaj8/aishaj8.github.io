The Napanee River and Wilton Creek watersheds are located in one of the most nutrient-impaired areas of the Great Lakes basin. This study adapts the SWAT process-based model with a reservoir-based representation to better capture the karst-influenced hydrology of these catchments. The calibrated framework is used to delineate spatial and temporal hotspots of excessive nutrient export and to evaluate the sensitivity of model predictions to different parameterization strategies.
