This book chapter provides a comprehensive treatment of Bayesian inference as a tool for uncertainty analysis in mathematical modelling of aquatic ecosystems. The authors discuss model calibration, parameter estimation, and predictive uncertainty quantification within a Bayesian framework, drawing on case studies from the Bay of Quinte and Lake Erie. The chapter addresses common challenges in applying these methods to complex environmental models, including structural uncertainty, data limitations, and the communication of probabilistic results to stakeholders and decision-makers.
