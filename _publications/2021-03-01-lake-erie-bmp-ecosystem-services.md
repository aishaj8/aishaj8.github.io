Lake Erie is the shallowest and most biologically productive system of the Great Lakes, surrounded by large urban, industrial, and agricultural areas. This paper critically evaluates nine agricultural BMPs and the modelling strategies used to quantify their effectiveness in reducing non-point source pollution. The study recommends dynamic representations of soil moisture, water table, nutrient availability, and plant growth interactions. It also advocates the expression of ecosystem services in monetary and non-monetary terms to improve policy trade-off analysis under the Integrated Watershed Management framework.
