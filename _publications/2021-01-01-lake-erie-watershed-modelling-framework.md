This paper highlights the establishment of a multimodel ensemble as a strategy to address conceptual and parametric uncertainty in watershed modelling. Using the Canadian side of the Lake Erie basin as a case study, the framework integrates multiple process-based and data-driven models to characterize water cycle dynamics and nutrient fate. The study argues that ensemble approaches improve the robustness of predictions used to inform adaptive management decisions, and provides practical guidance for watershed managers seeking to apply these tools.
