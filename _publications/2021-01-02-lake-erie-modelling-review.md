Elevated phosphorus loading into Lake Erie — particularly from agricultural and urban sources — is identified as a major driver of severe eutrophication. This review evaluates 11 process-based models for their ability to reproduce surface runoff, groundwater dynamics, sediment transport, nutrient cycling, and channel routing within a watershed context. The analysis finds that available models tend to trade off between simplified nonlinear dynamics and more advanced routing representations. The paper provides a road map for selecting robust and replicable modelling strategies for Lake Erie watershed management.
