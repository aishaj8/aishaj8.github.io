Freshwater ecosystems can experience harmful algal blooms that negatively impact recreational uses, aesthetics, taste, and odour in drinking water. Cyanobacterial toxins, particularly microcystins, can have serious repercussions on aquatic wildlife and human health. This study develops a Bayesian probabilistic framework to characterize the risk of microcystin violations under different environmental conditions in the Bay of Quinte. The model integrates water quality monitoring data and environmental covariates to estimate exceedance probabilities relative to Health Canada drinking water and recreational guidelines, offering a decision-support tool for water managers.
