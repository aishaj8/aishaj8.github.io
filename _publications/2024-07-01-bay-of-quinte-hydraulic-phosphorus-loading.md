This study focuses on the Bay of Quinte watershed on the northeastern shore of Lake Ontario, Canada. Special attention is paid to the Napanee River and Wilton Creek catchments, where subterranean conduit networks facilitate groundwater–surface water interactions that increase nutrient delivery to downstream water bodies. The work integrates historical records with current monitoring data and modelling projections to characterize how hydraulic and phosphorus loading patterns have changed over time and what future trajectories may look like under different management and climate scenarios.
