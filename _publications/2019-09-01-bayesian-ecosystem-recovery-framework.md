Evaluating the degree of improvement of an impaired freshwater ecosystem resembles null-hypothesis testing, where prevailing conditions are compared against a reference state. This study develops a Bayesian framework that quantifies the probability that an ecosystem has reached a recovery threshold, accounting for natural variability and monitoring uncertainty. The framework is applied to a Canadian Great Lakes case study and provides a principled, transparent basis for managers to determine when remediation targets have been sufficiently met — a critical step in Remedial Action Plans and adaptive management cycles.
